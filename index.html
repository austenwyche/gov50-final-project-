Project thoughts

I am interested in exploring data related to the relationship between gun ownership rates in the United States and the frequency of mass shootings. Does increased gun ownership have a positive relationship with the frequency of mass shootings, or is there no relationship between the two?

Milestone 2

Do gun ownership rates correlate with the number of mass shootings in the United States? In this study, I plan to analyze the relationship between self-proclaimed Gun Ownership in Gallup polling and the occurance of mass shootings (defined by a shooting where at least three deaths occur). I hypothesize there is likely no relationship between gun ownership rates and mass shootings. I expect that the rate of gun ownership in the United States has remained stable, while the rate of mass shootings has likely increased over the past couple of decades. My unit of analysis is the United States over the period 1980-2017. The explanatory variable of interest is the gun ownership rate, measured by public policy polling. The outcome variable is the number of mass shootings in the United States. The outcome variable of interest is measured by Mother Jones, a nonprofit organization which compiled records of mass shootings in the United States since the year 2000. A pattern that displays little to no correlation between gun ownership rates and mass shootings in the United States would confirm my hypothesis.

Introduction

The main research question is: What is the relationship between gun ownership rates and the number of mass shootings (per year) in the United States? My main hypothesis is that in years with higher gun ownership, there will be a larger number of mass shootings taking place. This is important to discover whether there is a correlation between allowing more people to own guns and more instances of violence.

In the United States, there is major debate over whether the gun ownership rate makes Americans safer or more vulnerable to danger. By analyzing the relationship between gun ownership and mass shootings, I hope to discover whether there is a relationship between the two.

Data Section

The main outcome of interest is the number of mass shootings in the United States per year. The outcome variable of interest is measured by Mother Jones, a nonprofit organization which compiled records of mass shootings in the United States since the year 2000.

As we can see below, the number of mass shootings in the United States has increased in frequency over the last four decades. One limitation of this data is the fact that some acts of gun violence (i.e.when there are only two victims) are not classified as mass shootings despite the weapon being a firearm. While this is a limitation of the data, the plot by Mother Jones data displays the number of mass shootings (defined as 3 or more killings in a single incident by United States statutes).
